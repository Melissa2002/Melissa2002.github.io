Machine Learning, EEG Signals, and Emotion

Volunteering at the University of California, San Francisco - The Abbasi Lab

Written on 05/02/2025

After interning with Surgalign Spine in 2023, I developed an interest in further exploring the nervous system and medical devices. This led me to exploring local opportunities, where I read about the Abbasi Lab, a group innovating and applying machine learning tools in order to analyze brain functions.

The project I am helping with aims to find connections between EEG data and positive human emotions using deep learning. It is our hope that this research will eluciadate some information behind brain activity, which is an underdeveloped area of study. So far, I have been primarily preprocessing EEG signals using MATLAB. Although I am relatively new to MATLAB, I find my coding skills from other languages are transferable. I also believe that MATLAB will be beneficial for my electrical and computer engineering master's coming up in the fall.
